Letters, Arts, and Sciences | las.psu.edu

The LAS degree within The College of Liberal Arts requires a degree proposal submission process from all of its students. With this site, the submissions went from the traditional mailing system to an online submission. The following is my list of actions as creator of the site:

  • Chose the technology: I researched possible solutions and went with a combination of WordPress and Penn State's Angel CMS.
  • Rethought the needs of LAS: We decided to make profile pages of staff and peers in order to address another issue within LAS: lack of relationship between students and staff since LAS is an online degree.
  • Gathered content: I attended meetings with World Campus university advisers to get people on the same page about the plan for the degree proposal process. I also communicated with advisers and potential mentors on a weekly basis in order to gather autobiographies, pictures, and other content. I took photos around campus and used one for the center display in the homepage.
  • Created: I used HTML and CSS to build the website out of the pre-packaged WordPress service for Penn State faculty and students (sites.psu.edu). This was actually the easiest part of the project.
  • Presented: I attended and presented the website to a panel of university world campus coordinators and directors.
